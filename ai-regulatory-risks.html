Analysis: November 3, 2025

Official Gazette of the Federation

Date: Monday, November 3, 2025
Scope: Federal
Sectors: Multi-sector (Fiscal, Automotive, Health, Manufacturing, Urban Development, Justice)

Risk Categories Identified in this Analysis

R1: Malfunctions & Errors R2: Discrimination & Bias R3: Privacy Invasions R7: Labor Displacement R9: Authoritarian Surveillance R13: Gradual Loss of Control

Synthesis

Document Analysis

The Official Gazette of the Federation of November 3, 2025 contains various publications from Secretariats and autonomous bodies. Although none of them explicitly mention 'Artificial Intelligence' or 'AI', an analysis reveals multiple contexts where its application is highly probable or imminent, generating important regulatory risks and significant gaps.

Critical Findings:

  • SAT - Security Codes: Automated security code system for tobacco products without AI governance framework
  • SAT - CFDI Validation: Verification platform with automated processing without algorithmic transparency
  • Mint House: Technological innovations for minting without AI policy or labor impact assessment
  • Health - Vulnerability Diagnosis: Diagnostic services for vulnerable populations without bias audits
  • BANAVIM - Gender Violence: Massive collection of sensitive data without specific AI safeguards
Main Recommendation: The identified regulatory gaps require immediate attention to establish AI governance frameworks in critical areas: taxation, national currency production, public health, and care for victims of gender violence. The absence of explicit mention of AI in high-risk contexts where its use is highly probable requires urgent action.

Detailed Findings

H1

SAT - Automated Access to Security Codes

Severity: Extreme Fiscal - Tobacco

Finding Type

Implicit - Technology

Original Fragment

"Online access: Entry available permanently, remotely and in an automated manner to the records of Security Codes printed on packs, cases, packages, wraps or any other object containing cigarettes or other manufactured tobacco except cigars and other manufactured tobacco made entirely by hand and to the information derived from the security codes."

Annex 26, I. Definitions, section 1

Identified Risks

R1 - Malfunctions & Errors

Errors in automated algorithms could cause incorrect validation decisions

High
R9 - Authoritarian Surveillance

Mass surveillance system without clear democratic controls

Very High
R13 - Gradual Loss of Control

Critical dependence on automated system without effective human supervision

Extreme

Analysis

The term 'automated' and the processing of 'derived information' from security codes in an online access system for the tobacco industry strongly suggests the use of advanced algorithms, possibly including AI/ML for fraud detection, pattern analysis or anomaly identification.

CRITICAL REGULATORY GAP: The absence of explicit mention of AI in such a critical system for fiscal and product control represents a significant regulatory gap. The opacity of these systems could generate distrust in the industry and make it difficult to challenge automated decisions.

Recommendations

  • Require transparency about the algorithms used for the generation and verification of security codes
  • Establish independent audit mechanisms to assess accuracy, fairness and robustness
  • Define clear protocols for review and appeal of automated decisions
  • Implement effective human supervision in critical processes
H2

SAT Verification Platform

Severity: Extreme Fiscal - Tobacco

Finding Type

Implicit - Technology

Original Fragment

"SAT verification platform: Set of SAT applications that allows verification of the validity of security codes through consultation of derived information."

Annex 26, I. Definitions, section 7

Identified Risks

R1 - Malfunctions & Errors High
R9 - Authoritarian Surveillance Very High
R13 - Gradual Loss of Control Extreme

Analysis

The 'SAT verification platform' is the component that allows verification of product authenticity. If it uses AI/ML to 'verify validity', the risks of malfunction, bias or surveillance are inherent. Validity decisions can have direct economic impacts on companies and consumer trust.

REGULATORY GAP: The ability of citizens to challenge decisions from an algorithmic 'black box' is limited.

Recommendations

  • Require transparency about security code verification algorithms
  • Establish independent audit mechanisms
  • Define review and appeal protocols
  • Implement effective human supervision
H3

Security Code System Validations

Severity: Extreme Fiscal - Tobacco

Finding Type

Implicit - Technology

Original Fragment

"Technical and security characteristics of the Security Code System... 2. Validation of required data. The mandatory fields of the Security Code schemas that comply with the defined data schema will be validated. 3. Additional validations. Applicable business rules will be validated. 4. Flow validation. Compliance with passage through each component that integrates the end-to-end service will be validated."

Annex 26, II. Technical and security characteristics

Identified Risks

R1 - Malfunctions & Errors High
R13 - Gradual Loss of Control Extreme

Analysis

The 'additional validations' and 'flow validation' in a system that handles a massive volume of security codes are areas where AI/ML could be implemented to detect complex fraud patterns that would not be evident with simple rules.

REGULATORY GAP: The description is generic, which hides the possible algorithmic complexity. If AI is used, the risks of validation errors or gradual loss of control are high.

Recommendations

  • Specify whether validations incorporate AI/ML algorithms
  • Establish governance framework for AI development and deployment
  • Guarantee explainability of automated decisions
H4

CFDI and CSD Validation Systems

Severity: Extreme Fiscal

Finding Type

Implicit - Technology

Original Fragment

"Update validation systems with LCO input. Perform validations regarding CSD status and those regarding obligation marks linked to RFC keys."

Annex 29, III.1 List of Obligated Contributors (LCO), B. Procedure, sections 5 and 6

Identified Risks

R1 - Malfunctions & Errors High
R3 - Privacy Invasions Very High
R9 - Authoritarian Surveillance Very High
R13 - Gradual Loss of Control Extreme

Analysis

The 'validation systems' for the List of Obligated Contributors (LCO) and the status of the Digital Seal Certificate (CSD) are fundamental to the fiscal operation of all companies in Mexico. It is highly likely they use AI/ML to detect inconsistencies, fraud or compliance risks.

CRITICAL REGULATORY GAP: Errors or biases in these algorithms could paralyze legitimate commercial operations, violate the privacy of massive fiscal data and generate high risk of abuse or inefficiency.

Recommendations

  • Require disclosure of whether systems use AI/ML
  • Implement bias and fairness audits
  • Establish clear process to challenge automated decisions
  • Guarantee security and privacy of fiscal data
H5

Information Processing - Volumetric Controls

Severity: Extreme Fiscal - Energy

Finding Type

Implicit - Technology

Original Fragment

"Information processing consists of subjecting the generated, collected and stored information to a series of programmed operations that allow:..."

Annex 30, 30.6.1.4. Information processing requirements

Identified Risks

R1 - Malfunctions & Errors High
R9 - Authoritarian Surveillance Very High
R13 - Gradual Loss of Control Extreme

Analysis

The 'programmed operations' for processing volumetric control information on hydrocarbons are a critical area where AI/ML could be used to detect anomalies, prevent fuel theft ('huachicoleo') or ensure proper taxation.

CRITICAL REGULATORY GAP: Errors in these systems could have a massive economic impact, affect national energy security and, in the Mexican context where fuel theft is a serious problem, opacity could generate distrust or be exploited.

Recommendations

  • Require specification of whether they include AI/ML algorithms
  • Establish transparency and auditability requirements
  • Develop human review and appeal mechanisms
  • Evaluate impact on operational safety and fraud prevention
H6

Mint House - Technological Innovations

Severity: Extreme Manufacturing

Finding Type

Implicit - Risk/Regulatory Gap

Original Fragment

"incorporating technological innovations that optimize minting processes and elevate quality standards."

Institutional Program of Casa de Moneda de México 2025-2030, 4.3 SUBSTANTIVE PROJECTS

Identified Risks

R1 - Malfunctions & Errors High
R7 - Labor Displacement Very High
R13 - Gradual Loss of Control Extreme

Analysis

The Casa de Moneda de México seeks 'technological innovations' to 'optimize minting processes'. In modern manufacturing, AI/ML is a key tool for process optimization (predictive maintenance, automated quality control, energy efficiency).

REGULATORY GAP: If AI is implemented without clear governance, there could be risks of errors in currency production, labor displacement due to automation, and gradual loss of control over complex systems in a critical State function.

Recommendations

  • Develop an AI policy for the Mint House
  • Conduct labor impact studies and retraining programs
  • Implement human supervision and 'kill switches' for critical systems
  • Evaluate dependence on external technology providers
H7

Mint House - Responsible ESG Minting

Severity: Extreme Manufacturing - Sustainability

Finding Type

Implicit - Risk/Regulatory Gap

Original Fragment

"PROMOTE RESPONSIBLE MINTING, BASED ON ESG CRITERIA (ENVIRONMENT, SOCIAL AND GOVERNANCE), WITH THE OBJECTIVE OF ACHIEVING SUSTAINABLE AND ENVIRONMENTALLY RESPONSIBLE PRODUCTION. Incorporate Life Cycle Analysis to develop sustainable production. Reduce carbon footprint in metallic currency production."

Institutional Program of Casa de Moneda de México 2025-2030, OBJECTIVE 2

Identified Risks

R1 - Malfunctions & Errors High
R7 - Labor Displacement Very High
R13 - Gradual Loss of Control Extreme

Analysis

The promotion of 'responsible minting' based on ESG criteria, the incorporation of 'Life Cycle Analysis' and 'carbon footprint reduction' are objectives that greatly benefit from AI/ML for resource optimization, environmental monitoring and energy efficiency.

REGULATORY GAP: The absence of explicit mention of AI in these sustainability strategies is a gap. If AI systems are used, there are risks of errors in measurements or decisions, and complexity could lead to loss of control.

Recommendations

  • Establish guidelines for ethical use of AI in ESG optimization
  • Ensure transparency in ESG reporting data and algorithms
  • Train personnel in supervision of AI systems applied to sustainability
H8

Diagnosis of Persons in Vulnerable Situations

Severity: Very High Public Health

Finding Type

Implicit - Risk/Regulatory Gap

Original Fragment

"Comprehensive services for Diagnosis of persons in vulnerable situations and conditions of health units implementing the MoASMI"

Modification Agreement Ministry of Health - State of Tabasco, Annex 4, section 3

Identified Risks

R1 - Malfunctions & Errors High
R2 - Discrimination & Bias High
R3 - Privacy Invasions Very High

Analysis

The 'Diagnosis of persons in vulnerable situations' is a task that, due to its complexity and data volume, is an ideal candidate for the use of AI/ML to identify patterns, predict risks or allocate resources.

CRITICAL REGULATORY GAP: In the health sector, especially with vulnerable populations, the risks of algorithmic bias are very high, potentially leading to discrimination in access to services. Processing highly sensitive health data without specific AI safeguards is concerning.

Recommendations

  • Require disclosure of AI/ML algorithm use in vulnerability diagnoses
  • Implement human rights impact assessments and bias audits
  • Establish robust safeguards for health data privacy and security
  • Define human review and appeal mechanisms
H9

Health Care Model with Inclusive Mechanisms

Severity: Very High Public Health

Finding Type

Implicit - Risk/Regulatory Gap

Original Fragment

"Measures the number of health units that implement the Health Care Model with Inclusive Mechanisms in their facilities and operation of health services"

Modification Agreement Ministry of Health - State of Tabasco, Annex 7, indicator 1.2.2

Identified Risks

R2 - Discrimination & Bias High
R3 - Privacy Invasions Very High

Analysis

The 'Health Care Model with Inclusive Mechanisms' (MoASMI) seeks to improve healthcare. If it uses AI to identify inclusion needs, personalize services or allocate resources, there is a significant risk of algorithmic bias if the data is not representative.

REGULATORY GAP: The lack of specific regulation for the use of AI in an inclusive health model is a gap, especially in a country with high digital inequality and technological access.

Recommendations

  • Develop ethical guidelines for AI use in inclusive health models
  • Conduct rigorous bias testing and establish continuous monitoring
  • Ensure transparency about how MoASMI uses data and algorithms

H11

BANAVIM - National Database on Gender Violence

Severity: Very High Justice - Gender Violence

Finding Type

Implicit - Risk/Regulatory Gap

Original Fragment

"Collaborate with 'MUJERES' providing quarterly information through the report called 'Report of Women Attended', of women, as well as their daughters and sons attended for the first time and in follow-up, as well as the services provided by the CJM. Collaborate by providing corresponding information regarding care for women victims of gender violence for the collection of information from the National Database and Information on Cases of Violence against Women (BANAVIM);"

Agreement Ministry of Women - Mexico City, FIFTH, subsections k and l

Identified Risks

R2 - Discrimination & Bias High
R3 - Privacy Invasions Very High

Analysis

The massive collection of sensitive data on 'women attended' and 'victims of gender violence' in the 'National Database and Information on Cases of Violence against Women (BANAVIM)' is an area of extremely high risk.

CRITICAL REGULATORY GAP: If AI/ML is used to analyze this information, the risks of algorithmic discrimination and privacy invasion are extremely high. Bias in algorithms could lead to revictimization, inadequate allocation of protection or perpetuation of inequalities. The lack of mention of AI and specific safeguards is a critical regulatory gap.

Recommendations

  • Require disclosure of whether AI/ML algorithms are used in BANAVIM
  • Implement mandatory human rights impact assessments and bias audits
  • Establish strict data privacy and security protocols
  • Develop human review and appeal mechanisms
  • Train personnel in ethical and responsible use of AI

Methodology

Objective

Identify and analyze implicit or explicit mentions of Artificial Intelligence in Official Gazette of the Federation documents, evaluating regulatory gaps and potential risks.

Analysis Process

  1. Search for explicit mentions: Direct identification of the term "Artificial Intelligence" or related terms
  2. Identification of implicit mentions: Detection of technology descriptions that typically use AI (optimization, prediction, automation)
  3. Risk assessment: Classification according to categories adapted from "What Risks Does AI Pose?" and "Future Risks of Frontier AI"
  4. Gap analysis: Identification of absences in regulatory frameworks, safeguards or supervision
  5. Generation of recommendations: Specific and actionable proposals

Severity Criteria

Extreme Critical risk to economic stability, national security or fundamental rights
Very High Risk of massive impact on fundamental rights or public safety
High Significant risk for vulnerable groups or critical sectors
Medium Moderate risk with possibility of mitigation
Low Minor or well-managed risk
